Metadata:
    author: Julie Clark
    description: A delicious way to use up sourdough discard! This soft Sourdough Pumpkin Bread recipe with cinnamon sugar topping makes two loaves.
    prep time: 15 minutes
    cook time: 1 hour
    yield: 2 loaves
    source: https://www.tastesoflizzyt.com/sourdough-pumpkin-bread/

Ingredients:
    baking powder                 1 tsp.
    baking soda                   1 tsp.
    brown sugar                   1 cup, packed
    cinnamon                      1 tbsp.
    cooking spray                 some
    flour                         405 grams
    granulated sugar              1.2 cups
    large eggs                    3
    pumpkin spice mix             2 tbsp.s
    pure pumpkin puree            2 cups
    salt                          1 tsp.
    sourdough discard             231 g grams
    vanilla extract               2 tsp.s
    vegetable oil                 0.75 cups

Cookware:
    oven
    large bowl
    loaf pan
    small bowl
    wire rack

Steps:
     1. Preheat the oven to 325F.
        [–]
     2. In a large bowl, combine the vegetable oil, large eggs, vanilla extract, granulated sugar, brown
        sugar, and pure pumpkin puree. Mix until well combined.
        [brown sugar: 1 cup, packed; granulated sugar: 1 cup; large eggs: 3; pure pumpkin puree: 2 cups;
        vanilla extract: 2 tsp.s; vegetable oil: 0.75 cups]
     3. Into the above mixture, fold in the sourdough discard.
        [sourdough discard: 231 g grams]
     4. Add in the flour, salt, baking powder, baking soda, and pumpkin spice mix until just combined.
        [baking powder: 1 tsp.; baking soda: 1 tsp.; flour: 405 grams; pumpkin spice mix: 2 tbsp.s; salt: 1
        tsp.]
     5. Spray the loaf pan with cooking spray.
        [cooking spray: some]
     6. Pour the batter evenly into the pans.
        [–]
     7. Mix the granulated sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
        [cinnamon: 1 tbsp.; granulated sugar: 0.25 cups]
     8. Sprinkle the cinnamon-sugar over the batter.
        [–]
     9. Bake for 60 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the loaf comes out clean.
        Internal temperature should be 190F.
        [–]
    10. Cool until the bread loosens from the pan evenly and turn onto a wire rack to cool completely.
        [–]
    11. Store in an airtight container.
        [–]
